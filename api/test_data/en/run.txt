===Etymology===
{{root|en|ine-pro|*h₃er-}}
From {{inh|en|enm|runnen}}, {{m|enm|rennen|t=to run}}, alteration (due to the past participle {{m|enm|runne}}, {{m|enm|runnen}},
{{m|enm|yronne}}) of {{cog|enm|rinnen|t=to run}}, from {{inh|en|ang|rinnan}}, {{m|ang|iernan|t=to run}} and {{der|en|non|rinna|t=to run}}, both from {{inh|en|gem-pro|*rinnaną|t=to run}}, from {{der|en|ine-pro|*h₃reyH-|t=to boil, churn}}.

Cognate with {{cog|sco|rin||to run}}, {{cog|fy|rinne||to walk, march}}, archaic {{cog|nl|rinnen}} (“to flow”, still in {{m|nl|geronnen}}), {{cog|de|rinnen|t=to flow}}, {{cog|sv|rinna|t=to flow}}, and {{cog|is|renna|t=to flow}}. From the causative {{m+|gem-pro|*rannijaną|t=to make run}} are {{cog|nl|rennen}}, {{cog|de|rennen}}, {{cog|da|rende}}, {{cog|sv|ränna}} (all “to run”). Non-Germanic cognates include {{cog|sq|rend||to run, run after}}. See also {{m|en|random}}.
